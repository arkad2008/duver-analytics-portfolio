## üìå Overview
This project analyzes customer behavior, marketing channels, and revenue attribution for **Showz**, an online store evaluating the effectiveness of its advertising campaigns. The goal is to determine which marketing sources generate profitable customers and whether the revenue from each sale covers the **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)**.

The analysis provides the company with clear insights to optimize marketing spend and maximize return on investment.

---

## üéØ Objectives

### **Main Objective**
Evaluate the performance of multiple marketing channels and determine whether the revenue generated by customers offsets the cost of acquiring them.

### **Specific Objectives**
- Clean and prepare customer datasets from various sources.
- Standardize variable names and handle inconsistencies.
- Analyze customer activity across marketing channels.
- Identify which channels bring the highest-value customers.
- Estimate the **Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)**.
- Compare LTV vs CAC to determine profitability.
- Provide actionable recommendations for marketing optimization.

---

## üìÇ Dataset
The project uses multiple raw data files from Showz‚Äôs internal analytics team.

The datasets include:
- **Customer IDs**  
- **Acquisition channels** (email, contextual ads, social media, etc.)
- **Traffic source & campaign information**
- **Purchase timestamps**
- **Revenue per user**
- **Cost of acquisition by channel**

These datasets were merged and cleaned to create a unified analytical table.

---

## üìä Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
The analysis explored:

- Customer distribution across marketing channels  
- Average revenue per user (ARPU) by channel  
- Time between acquisition and first purchase  
- Revenue concentration (Pareto-like behaviors)  
- Engagement metrics and sales frequency  

Visualizations included:

- Bar charts  
- Revenue distributions  
- Time-series plots  
- Channel-based comparisons  

---

## üìà Key Findings
- Some channels had **high acquisition volume but low revenue**, leading to negative ROI.  
- Others generated **fewer customers but much higher average revenue**, making them more profitable.  
- Several campaigns suffered from inconsistent naming, requiring cleaning before analysis.  
- Certain customer segments showed significantly higher repeat purchase rates.  

---

## üí∞ LTV vs CAC Analysis
The central business question: **Does the revenue per customer exceed the cost of acquiring them?**

Conclusions include:

- Some channels were **profitable**, with LTV > CAC.  
- Others were **operating at a loss**, requiring strategic intervention.  
- Increasing retention and repeat purchases could significantly boost LTV.  
- Channel performance varied widely depending on the campaign and traffic source.  

---

## üìù Conclusions
The analysis provides actionable insights for Showz, including:

- Invest more in channels with proven positive ROI.  
- Reduce or restructure campaigns that bring low-value customers.  
- Standardize marketing data naming conventions to avoid analytical inconsistencies.  
- Improve onboarding flows for high-acquisition channels to boost early revenue.

---

## üöÄ Next Steps
- Build a dashboard for real-time marketing analytics.  
- Apply more advanced LTV predictions using cohort analysis.  
- Perform A/B tests on marketing messaging.  
- Introduce automated cleaning pipelines for campaign data.
